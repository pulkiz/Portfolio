My Vision

As an engineer, I imagine a future where technology serves as a powerful enabler used to breaking barriers, expand opportunity, and address some of the most urgent challenges faced by society. My mission is to engineer systems that enhance Customer simplicity, expand financial access, and make digital tools more inclusive and accessible. I picture a world where a small business can grow online with confidence, without the worry of learning a lot to catch up, or where individuals in underserved communities gain financial freedom through intuitive, data-informed platforms. To me, engineering is not just about advancing technology, it’s about aligning innovation with human needs. I believe that progress should be driven by having a purpose, not just for money. By combining secure architecture with tools like machine learning and data analytics, I aim to create solutions that are both intelligent and equitable. Whether it’s enabling smarter urban infrastructure or developing sustainable energy networks, my focus remains grounded in real-world impact.

Agility and Adaptability

Goal 1

Over the next four months, I will improve my adaptability and productivity by refining my time management strategies. I will adopt the Time-Blocking Method, allocating focused blocks of time for coursework, work tasks, and study sessions. I will use Notion in combination with Google Calendar to visually organize and prioritize overlapping responsibilities. Success will be measured by meeting deadlines and figuring out my task completion rate. This will improve my adaptability to tools we use at the workplace, and also set a system up for me to not be overwhelmed by excess workloads.

Goal 2

Over the next year, I will improve my agility in learning by adopting a timed new-skill strategy, a method to increase my arsenal of tools I am fluent with to use in the workforce and for school. I will commit to learning and applying one new tool, platform, or framework every month (e.g., Docker, Excel VBA, REST APIs, New languages), dedicating at least two hours per week to hands-on experimentation and guided tutorials. This will help me grow my thinking, and not be surprised when seeing new technology that I would have otherwise been unprepared for.
